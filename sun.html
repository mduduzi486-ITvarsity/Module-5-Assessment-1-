The Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of the Solar System. It is a nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma, heated to incandescence by nuclear fusion reactions in its core, radiating the energy mainly as visible light and infrared radiation. It is by far the most important source of energy for life on Earth.

The Sun, like others stars, is a ball of gas. In terms of the number of atoms, it is made of 91.0% hydrogen and 8.9% helium. By mass, the Sun is about 70.6% hydrogen and 27.4% helium.

Sun Facts

  • Names: Sun, Sol /ˈsɒl/, Helios /ˈhiːliəs/
  • Surface area: 6.09×1012 km2; 12,000 × Earth
  • Escape velocity (from the surface): 617.7 km/s; 55 × Earth
  • Equatorial surface gravity: 274 m/s2; 28 × Earth
  • Mean radiance (Isol): 2.009×107 W·m−2·sr−1
  • Center density (modeled): 162.2 g/cm3; 12.4 × Earth
